Walking in Multiple Worlds (WiMW)

Walking in Multiple Worlds (WiMW) is an ITLCT program series (consisting of global workshops and speaker sessions starting in 2024) which aims to build awareness around the different ways in which native and indigenous peoples (both in urban and traditional homelands) interrelate and codeswitch in many facets of everyday life and how businesses and organizations can enhance understanding and allyship to build a more inclusive and understanding world (also with digital technology).

Land Acknowledgement

ITLCT operates predominantly through digital means. The Atlanta, Georgia area is the official home for ITLCT. Our leaders are located in the United States between the states of Georgia and Florida and so we wish to acknowledge both locations.

We acknowledge that ITLCT leaders reside on the traditional homelands and territories of Florida consisting of but not limited to the Seminole Nation and other first peoples such as the Calusa, Tobobaga, Timucua, Apalachee, Miccosukee, Mascogo, Ta√≠no, amongst others. ITLCT also acknowledge that we operate, and our leaders also reside on the traditional homelands and territories of Georgia consisting of the Cherokee Nation and other first peoples such as but not limited to the Catawba, Muscogee (Creek), Chickasaw, Shawnee, Oconee, Apalachicola, Chatot, amongst others.

The first peoples of Florida and Georgia who lived, worked, nurtured the land, and shared knowledge were involuntarily removed, faced difficult challenges and oppression, may still be seeking recognition and reconciliation in some cases, but today, they continue to shape their communities and the future in positive ways. ITLCT recognizes this and vows to be humble and respectful stewards of the lands, histories, and communities of Florida and Georgia's first people and other global tribes, nations, and communities as we engage in our work.
